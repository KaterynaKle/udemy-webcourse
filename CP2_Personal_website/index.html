Kea Nestor Notabilis

Hey! I'm Kea Nestor.
I'm mostly olive-green with a brilliant orange under my wings and have a large, narrow, curved, grey-brown upper beak.

Skills

The endangered kea is one of the most intelligent birds in the world. The Nestor notabilis parrot has skills such as problem solving, tool use, learning new skills by observation and spatial memory, all accompanied by a relative brain size similar to crows and primates. Kea's behavior is characterized by:

  • Patent curiosity towards humans and ski equipment.
  • Chewing the rubber parts of cars left in parking lots.
  • Opening the lids of garbage containers to obtain food scraps.

General skills

  • Keas are very intelligent and social birds.
  • These birds have remarkable dominance hierarchies.
  • The young birds learn new skills from older.
  • Nestor notabilis is particularly known for its ability to solve problems.

Specific skills

  • A kea learnt to turn on the water tap at Aspiring Hut campground.
  • A kea learnt to use tools to set off stoat traps to get the eggs.
  • A kea was seen having a tug-of-war with a cat over a rabbit carcass.
  • A kea that was being attacked by magpies hid behind a tramper who fended them off.

About Me

The Kea parrot is principally a mountain bird being found from around 600 – 2000 metres above sea level. During winter the Keas tend to spend most of their time at the lower altitudes where food is more plentiful, however, in spring and autumn they move up into the sub-alpine scrub and grassland to feed on seasonal fruit and berries.

Kea parrots prefer to spend most of their time on the ground entertaining humans with their side-hopping movements. However, when they are in flight they appear magnificent fliers. Kea Parrots like to get into buildings whatever way they can, even down chimneys. They make themselves welcome at ski lodges. Once inside buildings, nothing is sacred, if something can be chewed then they will have a go. The Kea parrots diet is quite varied including leaf buds, roots, berries, fruit, seeds, blossoms, nectar, carrion and insects. They are particularly fond of the nectar of flax, rata, snow totara and coprosma. The Keas long beak is a valuable tool in its search for food especially in crevices in between rocks and boulders and for prizing off the lids of rubbish bins.
